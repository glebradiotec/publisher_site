Electrodynamic analysis of microstrip antenna arrays for high-precision positioning

A. V. Bogoslovsky iD
Department of Radiophysics, Voronezh State University, Russia
D. N. Borisov iD
Institute of Applied Physics, Russian Academy of Sciences
Received: 12 Jan 2026 / Accepted: 05 Mar 2026 / Published: 15 Mar 2026

Abstract

This paper presents a comprehensive electrodynamic analysis of microstrip antenna arrays designed for high-precision positioning of mobile objects using Global Navigation Satellite System (GNSS) signals. We propose a novel approach to minimize multipath errors by optimizing the array geometry and employing a specialized substrate material with high permittivity. Numerical simulations using the Finite Element Method (FEM) demonstrate a significant improvement in the axial ratio bandwidth and phase center stability compared to conventional designs. Experimental results obtained from a prototype array confirm the theoretical predictions, showing a positioning accuracy improvement of up to 15% in challenging urban environments. The proposed antenna system is suitable for integration into autonomous vehicles and precision agriculture machinery.
